This is not just a budgeting tool decision. It is a decision about how NBPA Finance wants to operate going forward and how effectively the team can support leadership with timely, credible insight. The right platform means less time pulling reports together and more time helping the business make better decisions, with clearer visibility into tradeoffs and stronger transparency for stakeholders.

Several of the options can look similar at first because each offers some level of Excel familiarity. What matters most is not how polished the demo feels, but how well the solution holds up once Finance owns it. The key questions are whether the architecture is built to scale, whether reporting works the way Finance needs it to in practice, and how much maintenance the team takes on to keep the platform running well.

1

What NBPA Finance Needs from Its Next Platform

Management Reporting Agility

FP&A needs to build, refresh, and share management reports independently, without routing requests through Sage or the accounting team each cycle.

A Platform FP&A Can Own

With a lean team, the platform must be maintainable without IT involvement. Finance should control the model, manage cycles, and adapt reporting without outside help for routine tasks.

Scalable Foundation for Growing Complexity

As NBPA's structure and reporting needs evolve, the platform must scale cleanly across entities, dimensions, and use cases without creating new maintenance burden.

Low-Risk Implementation and Long-Term Fit

A lean finance team cannot absorb a difficult rollout or a platform that needs replacing in 18 months. Implementation quality and platform depth matter as much as features.

What matters after the demo

Architecture, maintainability, implementation quality, and long-term platform fit matter more than surface-level Excel familiarity. Several options can look compelling in a guided demo. The more important question is which one Finance can realistically own, maintain, and rely on month after month.

Where NBPA Finance Is Today and How to Evaluate the Options

What is getting in Finance's way today

  • Reporting still leans too heavily on Sage and the accounting team. Finance does not yet have the self-service reporting independence it needs
  • FP&A is spending too much time getting information and not enough time analyzing, advising, and acting on it
  • Time is lost requesting, rebuilding, and reinterpreting reports that should be available on demand
  • A lean team cannot afford another platform that adds admin complexity or becomes a short-term fit requiring replacement

What to pressure-test in the evaluation

  • How quickly can FP&A produce management-ready reports on its own, without routing requests through accounting or ERP exports
  • How much ongoing admin work sits behind the interface and does that burden grow as the business changes
  • How well does the platform scale as dimensions, entities, and reporting use cases evolve over time
  • How much implementation risk and post-go-live complexity is the team taking on relative to what it gains
What can look appealing early vs. What matters once the team owns it
  • Excel-familiar interface and recognizable layout
  • Polished dashboards and visual outputs
  • A setup story that sounds lighter or easier than it may be in practice
  • An initial workflow that feels simple in a guided demo setting
  • Management reporting agility without accounting dependency
  • Ongoing admin burden and maintainability for a lean team
  • Architecture that scales cleanly as complexity grows
  • Implementation quality and dedicated post-go-live support
  • Long-term platform fit without having to revisit the decision in 12 to 24 months

The Four Paths Under Evaluation

Martus

The incumbent. Martus supports parts of the current process, but it is not giving NBPA the reporting agility or self-service capability Finance is looking for. Staying put is the lowest-change path, and also the one most likely to preserve the same constraints that prompted this evaluation.

Datarails

Familiar on the surface, more complex underneath. Datarails relies on a relational file-and-table structure behind the scenes. What can feel simple at first can turn into more admin work over time as reporting and planning needs expand.

Cube

Lighter weight today, potentially limiting tomorrow. Cube can look appealing as an easier entry point, but it raises real questions about long-term depth, flexibility, and scalability for a growing FP&A function.

Vena  ✓ Recommended

A finance-owned platform that combines Excel familiarity with a purpose-built, multi-dimensional OLAP foundation for planning, reporting, and analysis. It is designed to reduce dependency, strengthen decision support, and give Finance a platform it can grow with.
